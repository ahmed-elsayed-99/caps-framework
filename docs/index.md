# CAPS Framework

**Competitive-signal Anchored Purchasing-power Segmentation**

CAPS is a Python library for inferring purchasing power patterns from behavioral
signals generated by competitive product launch events. It integrates entropy-weighted
dimensionality reduction with structured qualitative refinement to produce segment
profiles that are both statistically grounded and substantively interpretable.

## Why CAPS

Conventional purchasing power measurement relies on self-reported income, household
expenditure surveys, or demographic proxies. These instruments share a structural
limitation: they capture financial capacity as it is reported or historically recorded,
not as it manifests under genuine competitive pressure.

When a new product enters an established category, it creates a natural experiment
with unusually high discriminatory power. Consumers cannot avoid responding.
Each response pattern — adoption speed, switching direction, price sensitivity,
attribute priority, loyalty retention — encodes information about purchasing capacity
that no survey instrument would reveal with equivalent fidelity.

CAPS makes this inferential chain explicit, formal, and reproducible.

## Architecture

Competitive Launch Event
|
[Layer 1] Signal Acquisition
|  Multi-dimensional behavioral response matrix X (n x p)
|
[Layer 2] Behavioral Compression
|  Entropy weighting -> PCA -> Two-stage clustering -> Z (n x k)
|
[Layer 3] Qualitative Refinement
|  Purposive sampling -> Depth interviews -> Gioia coding -> Alignment
|
Purchasing Power Segment Profiles
